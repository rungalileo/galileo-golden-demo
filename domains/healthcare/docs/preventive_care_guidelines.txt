# Preventive Care and Screening Guidelines

## Adult Preventive Care Schedule

### Cardiovascular Risk Assessment

**Blood Pressure Screening**
- **Who:** All adults age 18 and older
- **Frequency:** 
  - Normal BP (<120/80): Every 2 years
  - Elevated BP (120-129/<80): Annually
  - Stage 1 HTN (130-139/80-89): Confirm within 1 month
  - Stage 2 HTN (≥140/90): Within 1 week or immediately if symptomatic
- **Method:** Office BP measurement; consider ambulatory or home BP monitoring
- **Notes:** Use proper technique (rested 5 minutes, correct cuff size, seated)

**Lipid Screening**
- **Who:** 
  - Men age 35+ and women age 45+
  - Younger if risk factors present (diabetes, smoking, family history)
- **Frequency:**
  - Normal lipids, low risk: Every 5 years
  - Borderline high or risk factors: Every 1-2 years
  - On treatment: Annually or per treatment protocol
- **What to test:** Total cholesterol, LDL, HDL, triglycerides
- **Interpretation:**
  - LDL <100 mg/dL (optimal)
  - HDL >40 mg/dL men, >50 mg/dL women
  - Triglycerides <150 mg/dL

**ASCVD Risk Calculator**
- **Who:** Adults age 40-75 without known ASCVD
- **Frequency:** Every 4-6 years
- **Purpose:** Guide statin therapy decision-making
- **Action:** Consider statin if 10-year risk ≥7.5%

**Diabetes Screening**
- **Who:**
  - All adults age 35-70 with BMI ≥25
  - Adults with risk factors at any age
- **Frequency:** Every 3 years if normal
- **Method:** Fasting glucose, HbA1c, or oral glucose tolerance test
- **Criteria:**
  - Prediabetes: FPG 100-125, HbA1c 5.7-6.4%
  - Diabetes: FPG ≥126, HbA1c ≥6.5%

**Abdominal Aortic Aneurysm Screening**
- **Who:** Men age 65-75 who have ever smoked
- **Frequency:** One-time screening
- **Method:** Abdominal ultrasound
- **Action:** Monitor or refer vascular surgery if AAA ≥5.5 cm

### Cancer Screening

**Colorectal Cancer Screening**
- **Who:** Average-risk adults age 45-75 (start earlier if family history)
- **Options and frequency:**
  - Colonoscopy: Every 10 years (preferred)
  - FIT (fecal immunochemical test): Annually
  - FIT-DNA (Cologuard): Every 3 years
  - CT colonography: Every 5 years
  - Flexible sigmoidoscopy: Every 5 years
- **Stop at:** Age 75 if normal screening, age 85 regardless
- **Notes:** Colonoscopy allows visualization and polypectomy

**Breast Cancer Screening**
- **Who:** Women age 40-74
- **Method:** Mammography
- **Frequency:**
  - Age 40-49: Shared decision-making (biennial vs annual)
  - Age 50-74: Every 2 years (USPSTF) or annually (American Cancer Society)
- **High risk:** Consider earlier screening and/or MRI
  - BRCA mutation carriers
  - Strong family history (2+ first-degree relatives)
  - History of chest radiation age 10-30
- **Clinical breast exam:** No longer routinely recommended
- **Self-exam:** Optional, not evidence-based but may promote awareness

**Cervical Cancer Screening**
- **Who:** Women age 21-65
- **Method and frequency:**
  - Age 21-29: Pap smear alone every 3 years
  - Age 30-65: Pap + HPV co-testing every 5 years (preferred) OR Pap alone every 3 years
- **Stop screening:** Age 65 if adequate prior screening and not high risk
- **Post-hysterectomy:** Discontinue if no cervix and no history of CIN2+ or cancer
- **Notes:** More frequent screening if immunocompromised or history of CIN2+

**Lung Cancer Screening**
- **Who:** Adults age 50-80 with 20+ pack-year smoking history who currently smoke or quit within past 15 years
- **Method:** Low-dose CT chest (LDCT)
- **Frequency:** Annually
- **Discontinue:** Once quit smoking for 15 years or life expectancy <10 years
- **Shared decision-making:** Discuss benefits and harms before screening

**Prostate Cancer Screening**
- **Who:** Men age 55-69 (controversial - shared decision-making required)
- **Method:** PSA blood test ± digital rectal exam
- **Frequency:** Every 2-4 years if screening elected
- **Considerations:**
  - African American men or family history: Discuss starting at age 45
  - Benefits: May detect early cancer
  - Harms: False positives, overdiagnosis, biopsy complications
- **Do NOT screen:** Men age >70 or life expectancy <10 years

**Skin Cancer Screening**
- **Who:** No clear guidelines for general population
- **High-risk patients:** Fair skin, multiple moles, family history, immunosuppression
- **Method:** Total body skin examination by dermatologist
- **Self-exam:** Monthly self-examination encouraged
- **ABCDE warning signs:**
  - Asymmetry
  - Border irregularity
  - Color variation
  - Diameter >6mm
  - Evolving/changing

### Infectious Disease Screening

**HIV Screening**
- **Who:** All adults age 15-65 at least once; earlier if sexually active
- **High risk:** Screen annually
  - Men who have sex with men
  - IV drug users
  - Sex workers
  - Multiple sexual partners
  - History of STIs
- **Pregnancy:** Screen all pregnant women
- **Method:** HIV antibody/antigen combo test

**Hepatitis C Screening**
- **Who:** 
  - All adults age 18-79 (one-time screening)
  - Anyone with risk factors (IV drug use, HIV+, incarceration)
  - Pregnant women at first prenatal visit
- **Method:** HCV antibody, confirm with HCV RNA if positive
- **Follow-up:** Treat all patients with chronic HCV

**Hepatitis B Screening**
- **Who:**
  - Pregnant women
  - High-risk individuals (IV drug use, dialysis, HIV+, immunosuppressed)
  - Foreign-born from endemic areas
- **Method:** HBsAg, anti-HBc, anti-HBs
- **Vaccination:** Offer to all susceptible adults

**Tuberculosis Screening**
- **Who:** Not routine for general population
- **Screen if:**
  - Close contact with active TB
  - Immunosuppressed (HIV, transplant, biologics)
  - Healthcare workers
  - Homeless or incarcerated
  - Foreign-born from high-prevalence countries
- **Method:** Tuberculin skin test (TST) or IGRA blood test
- **Follow-up:** Chest X-ray if positive test

**Sexually Transmitted Infections**
- **Chlamydia/Gonorrhea:**
  - All sexually active women age <25
  - Older women with risk factors
  - Men who have sex with men: Annually (test urine, throat, rectum)
  - Pregnant women at first prenatal visit
- **Syphilis:**
  - High-risk populations
  - All pregnant women
  - Men who have sex with men: Annually

### Osteoporosis Screening

**Who:**
- All women age ≥65
- Postmenopausal women age <65 with risk factors
- Men age ≥70
- Younger men with risk factors (steroids, hypogonadism)

**Method:** DEXA scan (dual-energy X-ray absorptiometry)

**Interpretation:**
- T-score ≥-1.0: Normal
- T-score -1.0 to -2.5: Osteopenia
- T-score ≤-2.5: Osteoporosis

**Frequency:**
- Normal bone density: Repeat every 10-15 years
- Osteopenia: Repeat every 2-5 years
- Osteoporosis: Monitor based on treatment

**Risk factors:**
- Low body weight (BMI <21)
- Smoking
- Excessive alcohol
- Glucocorticoid use
- Rheumatoid arthritis
- Parental hip fracture

### Mental Health Screening

**Depression Screening**
- **Who:** All adults, including pregnant and postpartum women
- **Frequency:** Periodically (at least at routine visits)
- **Tools:** PHQ-2 (initial), PHQ-9 (if positive)
- **PHQ-2 questions:**
  1. Little interest or pleasure in doing things?
  2. Feeling down, depressed, or hopeless?
- **Score:** ≥3/6 warrants full PHQ-9
- **Follow-up:** Ensure systems in place for diagnosis, treatment, and follow-up

**Anxiety Screening**
- **Who:** Adults age 19-64
- **Frequency:** Opportunistic screening
- **Tool:** GAD-7 (Generalized Anxiety Disorder-7)
- **Threshold:** Score ≥10 indicates probable anxiety disorder

**Alcohol Misuse Screening**
- **Who:** All adults age ≥18
- **Frequency:** Annually
- **Tools:** AUDIT-C or single-question screening
- **AUDIT-C:** Brief 3-question screen
  - Score ≥3 (women) or ≥4 (men) indicates risky drinking
- **Intervention:** Brief counseling for those with risky drinking

**Substance Use Screening**
- **Who:** Adults age ≥18
- **Frequency:** Opportunistic
- **Tool:** DAST-10 (Drug Abuse Screening Test)
- **Topics:** Marijuana, cocaine, opioids, stimulants, prescription misuse

### Immunizations (Adults)

**Influenza Vaccine**
- **Who:** All adults annually
- **Timing:** September-October (before flu season)
- **Type:** Age-appropriate vaccine
  - Age 18-64: Standard or high-dose
  - Age ≥65: High-dose or adjuvanted preferred

**COVID-19 Vaccine**
- **Who:** All adults age ≥18
- **Primary series:** mRNA vaccine (2 doses) or viral vector (1 dose)
- **Boosters:** As recommended by CDC (currently annually for age ≥65 or high-risk)

**Tdap/Td (Tetanus-Diphtheria-Pertussis)**
- **Tdap:** Single dose if not received as adult
- **Td:** Booster every 10 years
- **Pregnancy:** Tdap each pregnancy (27-36 weeks)

**Shingles (Herpes Zoster) Vaccine**
- **Who:** All adults age ≥50
- **Type:** Shingrix (recombinant) - 2 doses, 2-6 months apart
- **Note:** Give even if prior zoster infection or live vaccine

**Pneumococcal Vaccines**
- **Age ≥65:** 
  - PCV20 (one dose) OR
  - PCV15 followed by PPSV23 in 1 year
- **Age 19-64 with risk factors:**
  - Chronic lung/heart disease
  - Diabetes
  - Smoking
  - Alcoholism
  - Immunocompromised

**HPV (Human Papillomavirus) Vaccine**
- **Routine:** Age 11-26
- **Catch-up:** Age 27-45 (shared decision-making)
- **Series:** 2-3 doses depending on age at first dose

**Hepatitis A Vaccine**
- **Who:** 
  - All children age 1-18
  - Adults with risk factors or travel
  - Chronic liver disease
- **Series:** 2 doses, 6 months apart

**Hepatitis B Vaccine**
- **Who:** 
  - All adults age 19-59
  - Age ≥60 with risk factors
  - All adults with diabetes age <60
- **Series:** 3 doses (0, 1-2, 4-6 months)

**MMR (Measles-Mumps-Rubella)**
- **Who:** Adults born 1957 or later without evidence of immunity
- **Series:** 1-2 doses
- **Special:** Healthcare workers need 2 doses

**Varicella (Chickenpox)**
- **Who:** Adults without evidence of immunity
- **Series:** 2 doses, 4-8 weeks apart
- **Note:** Live vaccine - avoid in pregnancy and immunocompromised

### Lifestyle Counseling

**Tobacco Cessation**
- **Who:** All tobacco users
- **Frequency:** Every visit
- **5 A's:**
  - Ask about tobacco use
  - Advise to quit
  - Assess willingness
  - Assist with cessation plan
  - Arrange follow-up
- **Pharmacotherapy:** Consider nicotine replacement, varenicline, or bupropion

**Healthy Diet Counseling**
- **Who:** All adults, especially those with risk factors
- **Topics:**
  - Increase fruits and vegetables
  - Choose whole grains
  - Limit saturated fat and sodium
  - Consider Mediterranean or DASH diet

**Physical Activity Counseling**
- **Goal:** 150 minutes moderate aerobic activity per week
- **Strength training:** 2 or more days per week
- **Older adults:** Balance exercises to prevent falls

**Weight Management**
- **Who:** Adults with BMI ≥25
- **Screening:** Calculate BMI at every visit
- **Intervention:** Behavioral counseling, diet, exercise
- **Consider referral:** If BMI ≥30 or BMI ≥27 with comorbidities

**Folic Acid Supplementation**
- **Who:** All women planning or capable of pregnancy
- **Dose:** 400-800 mcg daily
- **Purpose:** Prevent neural tube defects

**Aspirin for ASCVD Prevention**
- **Consider:** Age 40-59 with ≥10% 10-year ASCVD risk (shared decision-making)
- **Do NOT use:** Age ≥70 or increased bleeding risk
- **Dose:** 81 mg daily

**Falls Prevention (Older Adults)**
- **Who:** Adults age ≥65
- **Screening:** Ask about falls in past year
- **Assessment:** 
  - Gait and balance evaluation
  - Home safety assessment
  - Medication review
  - Vision/hearing check
- **Interventions:**
  - Exercise programs (especially balance training)
  - Vitamin D supplementation
  - Remove home hazards

### Age-Specific Preventive Care Summary

**Ages 18-39 (Young Adults)**
- Blood pressure: Every 2 years
- Lipids: Starting age 35 men, 45 women (earlier if risk factors)
- Diabetes: Age 35+ if overweight
- Depression/anxiety: Periodically
- STI screening: If sexually active
- Tobacco cessation: All users
- Immunizations: Flu, COVID, Tdap, HPV (through age 26)

**Ages 40-64 (Middle Age)**
- Blood pressure: Annually
- Lipids: Every 5 years (more often if on treatment)
- Diabetes: Every 3 years
- Colorectal cancer: Starting age 45
- Breast cancer (women): Starting age 40-50
- Cervical cancer (women): Every 3-5 years through age 65
- Lung cancer: If 20+ pack-year smoking history
- Depression: Periodically
- ASCVD risk: Calculate every 4-6 years
- Immunizations: Flu, COVID, Tdap/Td boosters

**Ages 65+ (Older Adults)**
- Blood pressure: Annually
- Lipids: Annually if on treatment
- Diabetes: Annually
- Colorectal cancer: Through age 75 if not up to date
- Breast cancer (women): Through age 74
- Osteoporosis (women): Age 65+
- Depression: Periodically
- Falls risk: Annually
- Cognitive screening: Consider if concern
- Immunizations: Flu (high-dose), COVID, Td boosters, Shingrix, Pneumococcal

### Documentation and Follow-Up

**Preventive care visit should include:**
- Updated problem list
- Current medications and allergies
- Social history (smoking, alcohol, drugs, sexual activity)
- Family history (cancers, CVD, diabetes)
- Review of systems
- Age-appropriate screening tests
- Immunization status and updates
- Health maintenance plan
- Patient education and shared decision-making

**Use tracking systems:**
- Electronic health record reminders
- Disease registries
- Standing orders for preventive services
- Patient outreach for overdue screenings

**Overcoming barriers:**
- Cost: Preventive services covered by ACA with no copay
- Access: Use same-day appointments, walk-in clinics
- Patient knowledge: Provide clear education on benefits
- Time: Bundle multiple screenings in one visit when possible
